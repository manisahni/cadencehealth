Understanding GLP-1 Therapy

What is GLP-1?

GLP-1 (Glucagon-like peptide-1) is a hormone naturally produced in your intestine that helps regulate blood sugar and appetite. GLP-1 receptor agonist medications mimic this hormone to:

  • Slow gastric emptying, helping you feel full longer
  • Reduce appetite and food cravings
  • Regulate blood sugar levels
  • Improve insulin sensitivity
  • Promote weight loss

How Effective is GLP-1 Therapy?

Clinical studies have shown remarkable results:

  • Semaglutide: Average 15% weight loss
  • Tirzepatide: Average 22% weight loss
  • 68% of patients lose 10% or more of body weight
  • Sustained results with continued treatment
  • Additional benefits: Improved blood pressure, cholesterol, and diabetes control

GLP-1 Medications

Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy)

How it works:

  • Weekly injection
  • Reduces appetite and slows digestion
  • Regulates blood sugar
  • FDA-approved for weight management

Typical dosing schedule:

  • Week 1-4: 0.25mg weekly
  • Week 5-8: 0.5mg weekly
  • Week 9-12: 1.0mg weekly
  • Maintenance: 1.7-2.4mg weekly

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound)

How it works:

  • Weekly injection
  • Dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist
  • Enhanced weight loss compared to semaglutide
  • Improved glycemic control

Typical dosing schedule:

  • Week 1-4: 2.5mg weekly
  • Week 5-8: 5mg weekly
  • Week 9-12: 7.5mg weekly
  • Maintenance: 10-15mg weekly

Common Side Effects

Most side effects are mild and decrease over time as your body adjusts:

  • Gastrointestinal: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation
  • Other: Fatigue, headache, dizziness
  • Injection site: Mild irritation or redness

Management tips: Start with smaller meals, avoid fatty foods, stay hydrated, and take medication as prescribed.

Lifestyle Guidelines for Success

Nutrition Recommendations

Eat smaller, more frequent meals

GLP-1 medications slow gastric emptying, so smaller portions will help reduce nausea and optimize digestion.

Focus on protein

Aim for 25-30g protein per meal to maintain muscle mass during weight loss and enhance satiety.

Stay hydrated

Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily. Proper hydration helps manage side effects and supports metabolism.

Exercise Guidelines

Start gradually

Begin with 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week. Walking, swimming, and cycling are excellent options.

Include strength training

2-3 resistance training sessions per week help maintain muscle mass during weight loss.

Listen to your body

Adjust intensity based on how you feel. Some fatigue is normal as your body adapts to the medication.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly will I see results with GLP-1 therapy? +

Most patients begin to see weight loss within the first 4-8 weeks of treatment. However, significant results typically become apparent after 12-16 weeks as your dose is gradually increased. The medication works progressively, with continued improvement over 6-12 months.

What should I do if I experience nausea? +

Nausea is common but usually improves within 2-4 weeks. To manage nausea: eat smaller meals, avoid fatty or spicy foods, stay hydrated, and take your medication at bedtime. If nausea persists or is severe, contact your physician about adjusting your dose.

Can I stop taking the medication once I reach my goal weight? +

GLP-1 medications are typically used as long-term therapy. Studies show that weight regain commonly occurs when the medication is discontinued. Most patients continue on a maintenance dose to sustain their weight loss results. Your physician will work with you to determine the best long-term plan.

Will my insurance cover GLP-1 medications? +

Insurance coverage varies significantly by plan and indication. Coverage is more likely if you have diabetes or meet specific BMI criteria. We provide detailed documentation and can assist with prior authorization requests. Many patients use HSA/FSA funds for our services even if insurance doesn't cover the medication directly.

Are there any foods I should avoid while on GLP-1 therapy? +

While there are no strictly forbidden foods, you may find that high-fat, greasy, or very sweet foods worsen nausea and other side effects. Many patients naturally gravitate toward healthier options as their appetite and cravings change. Focus on lean proteins, vegetables, and whole grains for optimal results.

How do I store and inject my medication? +

Store your medication in the refrigerator (36-46¬∞F). Most GLP-1 medications come in pre-filled pens for easy injection. Inject subcutaneously (under the skin) in your thigh, abdomen, or upper arm. Rotate injection sites to prevent irritation. Your medication will come with detailed injection instructions and video tutorials.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Call 911 immediately if you experience:

  • Severe abdominal pain that doesn't improve
  • Signs of pancreatitis (severe stomach pain, nausea, vomiting)
  • Signs of severe dehydration
  • Allergic reaction symptoms (rash, difficulty breathing, swelling)
  • Persistent vomiting preventing fluid intake
